Outward 2 beta players are adjusting to major progression changes in the latest build. Recent reports from the community highlight new requirements to access magic training at the Leyline, alongside challenges in completing the second-tier combat exercises required to unlock high-tier skills. 

### Magic Training Now Gated by Mastat Ruins Quest

In earlier beta builds, visiting the Leyline in the Mastat ruins allowed players to acquire mana and buy magic skill books relatively early. However, recent patch updates have restricted this route. 

Community reports show that an iron gate now blocks the basement entrance to the Leyline, and the door to the top of the tower remains locked. Players attempting to bypass these barriers have confirmed that access is now tied directly to a quest. While the patch notes confirmed this change, the exact quest giver and prerequisites for unlocking the Mastat ruins remain unconfirmed. 

For those planning magic builds, this change means you can no longer rush directly to the Leyline for quick spellcasting setups. You will need to locate the correct questline in the local area first. Furthermore, keep in mind that nearby hostile mobs, such as ghosts wandering the mountain paths, can accidentally kill guarding NPCs if lured too close, which might complicate your access.

### The Grindy "Block Impact" Exercise

Another point of discussion in the community is the "exercise" system, a new progression mechanic in Outward 2 that players must complete to access higher skill tiers. To unlock Tier 3 skills, players must first clear all Tier 2 exercises. 

One specific task—blocking impact damage—is causing frustration. Several players have reported that even after blocking heavy attacks for several minutes using shields or weapons, the progress counter increases at an incredibly slow rate. 

While the exact formula for what counts as "impact damage blocked" is not yet clear, players are speculating on how to speed up the process:

*   **Shield vs. Weapon Block:** It is currently unconfirmed if weapon blocks progress the counter at the same rate as shield blocks, or if shield stats affect the progression speed.
*   **Perfect Blocks:** Some players suspect the exercise might favor active or timed blocks rather than holding the block button down continuously.
*   **Impact Stats:** The speed of progression might scale with the enemy's raw impact stat rather than their physical damage. Finding enemies that deal high posture and stability damage could be key.

As the Outward 2 beta continues to receive balance patches, these mechanics and progression rates are highly likely to change. Players hoping to test magic builds or heavy defense setups should keep an eye on upcoming hotfixes to see if developer Nine Dots Studio adjusts the requirements for these systems.
