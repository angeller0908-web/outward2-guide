If your RPG history consists of conquering Elden Ring or completing Baldur's Gate 3, stepping into the wilderness of Aurai can feel like running into a brick wall. The Outward series has always rejected the standard power fantasy, and the upcoming *Outward 2* is doubling down on this philosophy. Many players participating in recent playtests, as well as newcomers asking for Outward 2 tips, have noted how easy it is to feel lost when trying to play the game like a traditional action-RPG. 

To survive the harsh mechanics of *Outward 2*, you must change how you view progression, combat timing, and the role of magic.

### Story vs. Survival: Where to Start

A common issue raised by community members is whether to follow the main story immediately or spend time grinding for money and gear. In most RPGs, following the main questline is the safest path to level up. In *Outward 2*, that approach will likely get you killed. 

You play as an ordinary person, not a chosen hero. There are no traditional character levels to increase your base statistics. Your progression is tied entirely to your gear, your silver, and the skills you purchase from trainers. 

When starting out, focus on establishing a basic survival loop. Gather clean water, secure food, craft basic clothing, and obtain a reliable backpack. Do not rush out into major quest zones. Instead, explore the immediate surroundings of your starting settlement. Gather materials to sell, build up a reserve of silver, and invest in passive skills or basic active moves before committing to major journey paths.

### Overhauled Combat: Smoother But Still Relentless

For *Outward 2*, developer Nine Dots Studio has rebuilt the combat to feel more responsive. A major change confirmed for the sequel is the ability to cancel your attack animations into blocks or dodges. In the original game, committing to a swing meant you were completely vulnerable until the animation finished. 

While these combat upgrades make the game feel modern, the encounter design remains punishing. Playtesters from the recent Outward 2 beta have reported that human enemies behave with intense aggressiveness. Heavy two-handed weapons can feel slow and difficult to time against quick opponents, leading some community members to speculate that single-handed weapons paired with shields or fast dual-wielding setups are currently safer options.

To win fights, you must treat combat as a puzzle. Do not rely solely on raw reflexes. Use the environment to your advantage. Lay down tripwire traps before alerting a group of bandits. Drink elemental resistance potions (boons) before confronting magical beasts. Staggering enemies remains key to creating openings, and managing your stamina is far more important than landing a high-damage hit.

### The Truth About Magic: Not a Simple Path

New players often ask if magic is the best archetype to pick, assuming it offers an easier path through the game. While magic in *Outward 2* is incredibly powerful and versatile, it is far from a standard spellcasting experience.

To obtain mana in the first place, you must find specific locations and sacrifice portions of your maximum health and stamina. Furthermore, casting spells is not a simple matter of pressing a button to shoot fireballs. The system relies heavily on ritual combination. You must place physical sigils on the ground—such as a Fire Sigil using Fire Stones—and then cast secondary spells while standing inside them to trigger powerful elemental reactions. 

If you attempt to rush into a magic build without first securing a steady supply of reagent stones, proper mana-regenerating foods, and defensive equipment, you will struggle to survive. Magic is highly rewarding, but it is an endgame investment rather than a quick way to bypass the game's early difficulty.

Ultimately, success in the *Outward 2* beta and the upcoming release relies on preparation. Stop worrying about your character sheet and start focusing on what is in your backpack.
