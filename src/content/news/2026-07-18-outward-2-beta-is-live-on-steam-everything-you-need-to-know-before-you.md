# Outward 2 Beta Is Live on Steam: Everything You Need to Know Before You Load In

The Outward 2 beta is currently available on Steam, and if you were waiting for a sign to jump in, this is it. Nine Dots Studio, the Montreal-based indie team behind the original Outward, has opened access to their follow-up survival RPG through Steam's beta program. Whether you're a returning fan of the first game or someone looking for a survival RPG that actually means it, here's what you need to know.

## How to Access the Outward 2 Beta

Head to the Steam page for Outward 2 (App ID: 2849490 — note this is the beta build, separate from the eventual full-game listing). The official Nine Dots Studio website at [ninedotsstudio.com/outward-2](https://www.ninedotsstudio.com/outward-2) links directly to this beta page with a prominent "Play the beta now" button. If you're already on their site, that's your fastest route in.

The studio is also running a playtest registration page at ninedotsstudio.com/playtest-registration for players who want to be notified of upcoming sessions or waves.

## What Is Outward 2?

Outward 2 is a survival RPG set in Aurai, the same fantasy world introduced in the original Outward. The official description from Nine Dots is direct: it's a game about exploring, fighting, crafting, and adventuring, either alone or in co-op, at your own pace.

What makes it stand out from most RPGs is the studio's stubborn refusal to hold your hand. The original Outward built a reputation — sometimes infamous, sometimes beloved — for making players feel like actual fallible travelers rather than chosen heroes. Outward 2 carries that forward. There are no leveled encounters that scale to keep things comfortable, no divine gifts that make you overpowered, and no quest markers screaming at you. The game's tagline sums it up cleanly: "In Aurai, preparation is the key to survival."

## Core Mechanics and Design Philosophy

What we know from official sources about the gameplay:

**Survival as a foundation, not a gimmick.** Eating food, managing your stamina and resources, setting up camp — these aren't optional minigames. They're how the game works. If you ignore survival mechanics in Outward 2, Aurai will make you regret it.

**Seasonal changes.** Nine Dots has confirmed that the world adapts to changing seasons, and players are expected to adapt with it. What gear works in summer might not cut it in winter. Preparation for environmental shifts is likely to be a core planning consideration, though detailed mechanics around how seasons affect gameplay specifically have not been fully documented in available sources.

**Combat customization.** The studio's wording — "shape your own combat style" — suggests a flexible build system, though specific details on skill trees, weapon types, or magic systems have not been fully detailed in currently available materials. Players familiar with the first game's approach to "breakthrough" skill systems should expect something in that spirit, but it's worth waiting for more detailed beta coverage before drawing hard conclusions.

**Solo and co-op.** Outward 2 supports both single-player and cooperative multiplayer, confirmed by the official site. The original Outward's co-op was notably unusual — each player had their own independent story progression rather than one person "joining" another — and while it's reasonable to expect Nine Dots to refine that approach, the exact co-op structure for the sequel has not been detailed in currently available sources.

**Performance options.** Screenshots on the official site include a "POTATO MODE" label, suggesting Nine Dots is again including low-spec performance modes. The original Outward had broad hardware compatibility, and this seems to continue that tradition.

## What to Expect Going In Cold

If you've never played the original Outward, a few things will feel unusual by RPG standards:

**There is no retry button after death.** Or rather, dying has consequences but isn't necessarily a hard game-over. In the original, death put you in situations — captured, lost, unconscious somewhere unexpected — rather than just resetting you to a save point. Expect something similar philosophically.

**The map is not your guide.** Outward games are deliberately vague with navigation. You have a map, but you're expected to read terrain, landmarks, and your surroundings. Getting lost is part of the experience.

**Inventory and weight management matter.** Your backpack is a physical object that can be dropped in combat. What you carry, and how much of it, affects how you move and fight.

**The world doesn't wait for you.** Time passes, seasons change, and NPCs don't stand around indefinitely waiting for you to complete their quests. This creates a world that feels alive but also unforgiving if you fall behind on preparation.

## Tips for Beta Players

Since detailed patch notes and beta-specific guides are still emerging, the following is based on established knowledge from the first game and the studio's stated design philosophy:

**Feed yourself before heading out.** Food buffs in Outward carry real strategic value. Cooking meals rather than eating raw ingredients usually provides better stat bonuses. Get into the habit of preparing food before long expeditions.

**Don't sleep on your backpack.** The inventory system rewards deliberate packing. Carry what you need, not everything you find. A lighter pack means better mobility. A stuffed one means you'll be sluggish when it matters most.

**Talk to everyone.** Outward games tend to bury useful information — locations, warnings, quest context — in NPC dialogue that easy to skip. Take time early on to explore conversational options.

**Bring a friend if you can.** Even if you prefer solo play eventually, running the early sections of Outward with a second player is a genuinely different experience and can ease the learning curve significantly. If Outward 2's co-op follows the first game's model, both players will have their own progression, making it a proper co-op game rather than one person tagging along.

**Accept that you will fail.** The game is designed around setbacks. Getting knocked out, losing supplies, taking a wrong path — these are features, not failures. Learning the world through mistakes is the intended progression loop.

## What's Next

Nine Dots Studio has not announced a specific content roadmap for the beta period. The playtest registration page suggests there may be structured waves or updates. Watch the official site and the Steam community hub for Outward 2 (App ID 2849490) for patch notes and session announcements.

If you're the kind of player who has spent years wishing mainstream RPGs would actually let survival mechanics bite back — Outward 2's beta is worth a look right now.
